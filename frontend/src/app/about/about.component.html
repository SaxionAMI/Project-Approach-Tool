About



What is it?

The Project Approach Tool has been developed to help students determine their project approach. This can be done both top-down (from phases to questions, methods and

deliverables) and bottom-up (from deliverables and methods to questions and phases). The tool also supports online collaboration.


What is it based on?

The Project Approach Tool contains various card decks which can be used for inspiration. CMDmethods, ICT research methods and Engineering methods are all based on

the DOT framework and are grouped based on its field, library, workshop, lab and showroom strategies plus stepping stones.

The tool also contains the more generic stepping stones developed by Miriam Losse, which is used in 3S projects at Saxion.


Who is involved?

This tool has been developed by students, so far: Matyas Kone (Saxion HBO-IT), Lars Hendriks (Saxion HBO-ICT).

The development is monitored by a steering committee consisting of Danny Plass (project leader, Saxion HBO-ICT), Miriam Losse (Saxion Research in Education), Etto

Salomons (Saxion HBO-ICT, 3S), Kasper Kamperman (Saxion CMGT), Koen van Turnhout (HU, Human Experience & Media Design), and Sonja Rouwhorst (HvA CMD).

The project is partially funded by HBO-i.
